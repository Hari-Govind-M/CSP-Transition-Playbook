Orientation · read this first

What this transition actually is

In Microsoft's Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) program, partners resell Microsoft cloud services (Microsoft 365, Azure, Dynamics 365 and more) to their customers. There are two ways to do that — and this playbook moves a partner from the first model to the second.

Direct‑Bill Partner "Tier 1"

  • Buys cloud products directly from Microsoft and bills customers itself.
  • Owns the full stack: billing, invoicing, customer support, compliance, API integration.
  • Must keep meeting Microsoft's requirements, including ≥ USD $1 million in CSP annual revenue over the trailing 12 months, an eligible support plan (Advanced Support for Partners or Premier), Microsoft's security requirements (MFA for admins, a named security contact, 24‑hour alert response), and at least one Solutions Partner designation.

Indirect Reseller "Tier 2"

  • Buys cloud products through an Indirect Provider (an authorized distributor) instead of from Microsoft directly.
  • The provider handles billing with Microsoft, can purchase products for your customers, and supplies support infrastructure, training, marketing and credit terms.
  • No direct billing relationship with Microsoft and no revenue minimum — the partner keeps the customer relationship and keeps selling.
Why partners transition: most commonly because they no longer meet (or don't want to maintain) the direct‑bill requirements above. If a direct‑bill partner doesn't meet them, Microsoft requires the partner to transition to indirect reseller status to stay authorized to transact in CSP. Some partners also transition voluntarily to reduce operational overhead.

Who is who · responsibilities at a glance

The five players in every transition

Every action in this playbook belongs to exactly one of these five parties. If you remember nothing else, remember this section.

Direct‑Bill Partner (the transitioning partner)

Owns and executes the transition. All Partner Center actions happen in the partner's own tenant under their own credentials: checking status, enrolling as an indirect reseller, accepting the Microsoft Partner Agreement (a legally binding act, performed by a Global admin), inviting customers, and accepting/submitting subscription transfers (performed by an Admin agent). The partner also owns all customer communication — including the contractual obligation to inform customers and obtain their consent before extending a reseller relationship to a provider.

PTC Consultant

Guides, plans and supports — but does not act inside the partner's tenant. The PTC advises the partner on the right path, sequences the work, explains requirements, helps unblock issues, and escalates to the PTC CSP Community when something falls outside this playbook. Think of the PTC as the navigator; the partner drives.

Indirect Provider (distributor)

Becomes the partner's commercial backbone after the transition. The provider sends the partnership invitation that links the two accounts in Partner Center, submits orders on customers' behalf, owns the billing relationship with Microsoft, creates the transfer requests for existing subscriptions, attests customer acceptance of the Microsoft Customer Agreement where needed, creates Entra tenants for brand‑new customers (indirect resellers can't), and provides onboarding, support, training, marketing help and credit terms.

Customer

Must say yes — twice. Each customer (1) accepts the reseller relationship with the partner and the indirect provider, and (2) must have a valid acceptance of the Microsoft Customer Agreement (MCA) recognized through the indirect provider. Without these, the provider cannot submit orders or transfers for that customer.

Microsoft

Verifies, approves and governs. Microsoft verifies business details (account vetting), approves indirect reseller enrollment (usually immediate, up to 5 business days), changes a direct‑bill account from Active to Restricted on request via a support ticket, sends the incentives invitation, and publishes the agreements (MPA/MCA) and program rules that make the whole thing legally valid.

What the status means for your path

RESTRICTED → Path A · same tenant

You can enroll as an indirect reseller on the existing direct‑bill tenant. One tenant runs both the legacy direct business (wind‑down) and the new indirect business. This is the standard route once status is Restricted — Microsoft only allows same‑tenant enrollment in Restricted status.

ACTIVE → Path B · new tenant (or convert to Restricted)

Two options: (B1) create a new Entra tenant + new business location/PartnerID and enroll that new tenant as the indirect reseller — direct‑bill keeps running on the old tenant during migration. (B2) ask Microsoft Support to move the account from Active → Restricted, then follow Path A on the same tenant.

Understand "Restricted" before requesting it. Restricted direct‑bill means the partner can no longer grow its direct business (e.g., new customer acquisition and new direct orders stop) — existing subscriptions continue. Review restricted direct‑bill capabilities with the partner before choosing option B2.
Microsoft's own recommendation: keeping two separate tenants (one direct‑bill, one indirect reseller) is highly recommended, because enrolling in direct CSP on an indirect reseller tenant overrides the indirect reseller features. Factor this in if the partner plans to ever return to direct‑bill.

Phase 4 · the legally sensitive phase — consent and agreements

Connect existing customers to the provider

The provider cannot submit orders or transfers for a customer until a reseller relationship exists between that customer and the provider. For each existing direct‑bill customer there are two official ways to create it — choose per customer.

Method 1 · Reseller relationship extension

  • Extends your existing direct‑bill customer relationships to the provider via a templated email with a unique acceptance link.
  • Only available to direct‑bill partners who completed IR enrollment (Phase 2), only for existing direct‑bill customers, and only with providers whose invitation you accepted (Phase 3).
  • A copy of the customer's bill‑to information is shared with the provider.
  • The provider gets no admin privileges in the customer tenant.

Method 2 · Indirect reseller invitation

  • Invites the customer to establish a reseller relationship with you and the provider at the same time via a personalized link in a draft email you can edit (keep the link!).
  • Works for existing direct‑bill customers and new customers with an existing Entra tenant.
  • Use this method when the provider needs delegated admin access (GDAP) — the extension can't grant it.
  • After acceptance, you can manage the customer's subscriptions, licenses and users; the provider can submit orders.
⚖️ Contractual obligation — read this twice. Before using the reseller relationship extension, the partner is contractually obliged by the partner agreement to inform the customer and obtain their consent to establishing the relationship between that customer and the indirect provider. Using the extension also constitutes consent to sharing the customer's bill‑to information with the provider. Document the customer's consent before clicking.

How to run Method 1 (extension)

  1. Sign in to Partner Center as an Admin agent Partner and open the Customers workspace.
  2. In the Customer list, select New relationship from the action menu.
  3. A templated email opens with a unique acceptance link — send it to the customer (after consent is on record).
  4. The customer accepts Customer — they're authorizing you as their local reseller of record; the provider then appears as a CSP partner on the customer's Microsoft 365 Admin Center Partner relationships page.

How to run Method 2 (invitation)

  1. Sign in as Admin agent Partner → Customers → Invite new customers (for brand‑new IR customers, start from Indirect providers → Invite new customers instead).
  2. Review the draft email; edit the wording if needed but never remove the link — it's personalized to connect the customer to both your account and the provider's account.
  3. Select Done and send the email.
  4. The customer accepts Customer, authorizing you and the provider as resellers of record.
  5. Request GDAP (granular delegated admin permissions) to manage the customer's subscriptions, licenses and users on their behalf — the customer approves the GDAP relationship.
Brand‑new customers (no tenant yet): indirect resellers can't create Entra tenants for customers in Partner Center. The provider creates the tenant and names you as the indirect reseller, which makes the customer appear in your customer list. Also: you can't use leftover direct‑bill capability to purchase for customers acquired as an indirect reseller.

Phase 5 · move the billing, keep the customer

Transfer subscriptions to the indirect provider

Indirect resellers have no billing relationship with Microsoft, so every subscription the partner holds as direct‑bill must be transferred to the indirect provider's billing account using the self‑serve subscription transfer in Partner Center.

Prerequisites — do not skip: for each customer, (1) the customer has a reseller relationship with the indirect provider (Phase 4), and (2) the customer's MCA acceptance is in place through the provider. Transfers can't be tested in sandbox.

Only new commerce license‑based and Azure types can be transferred between transacting CSP partners. If the partner still holds legacy offers:

Phase 6 · life during and after the transition

Operating in dual mode, then as a pure indirect reseller

The transition isn't a hard cut‑over. While it runs, the partner is both a direct‑bill partner and an indirect reseller, and manages each customer set differently.

Tasks you keep doing as direct‑bill (until transferred)

  • Purchase / create orders for direct customers' products.
  • View subscriptions and manage order history.
  • Bill direct customers yourself, as before.
  • View/download CSP price lists; use sandbox & APIs; add new customer records.

Tasks you do as an indirect reseller

  • Request that the provider orders products for your customers.
  • Manage customers' licenses and users (with GDAP).
  • Create service requests on customers' behalf.
  • View customer analytics.

How to tell which kind a customer is

In Partner Center → Customers, open a customer's details. If you can see their subscriptions plus Add / View products options, you acquired them as direct‑bill. If those options aren't available, the customer is an indirect‑reseller relationship.

Capabilities you lose after transitioning: the sandbox and Partner Center API/SDK commerce capabilities are direct‑bill only. Plan any storefront/automation replacements with the provider before the direct‑bill side winds down.

Incentives

  • After enrolling as an indirect reseller on the existing direct‑bill tenant, the partner receives an invitation to the Indirect Reseller Incentive program within 30 days Microsoft sends.
  • The invitation is tied to the Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program (MAICPP) account associated with the CSP tenant, and goes to that account's registered email — make sure it's monitored.
  • The partner remains eligible for direct‑bill incentives on the same tenant during the transition, but the two programs must be managed separately.

Source: Transition guide — "Enroll for indirect reseller incentives"

Thinking of going back to direct‑bill one day?

  • There's no "conversion" back — the partner re‑applies for direct‑bill using a new tenant after meeting all requirements again (including the $1M trailing‑12‑months CSP revenue, earned as an indirect reseller).
  • A partner that transitioned (or was deauthorized / not approved) must wait one year before reapplying for direct‑bill status.
  • Customers can later be moved from the indirect tenant to the new direct‑bill tenant.
  • This is why Microsoft recommends two separate tenants: enrolling direct CSP on an indirect reseller tenant overrides the indirect reseller features.

Frequently asked questions

How long does the whole transition take?

It depends on the path and the customer count. Known timelines from the official process: indirect reseller enrollment approval is usually immediate but can take up to 5 business days; account verification for a new tenant typically takes around 2–3 weeks; the incentives invitation arrives within 30 days of IR enrollment. Customer relationship acceptance and subscription transfers depend on how quickly customers respond — plan weeks to months for a large book of business.

Do customers lose service during a transfer?

The transfer mechanism creates the new subscription on the provider's billing account and the system cancels the old one — it's a billing‑side move designed for continuity, not a service teardown. Still, coordinate timing, renewal dates and any provisioning specifics with the indirect provider per subscription, and communicate the change to the customer first (for the relationship extension, informing the customer and getting consent is contractually required).

Does the partner lose its customer relationships?

No. The customer remains the partner's customer; the partner keeps managing licenses, users and support (via GDAP). What changes is the billing chain: Microsoft bills the provider, the provider bills the partner, the partner bills the customer per their own commercial agreement.

Can the partner run direct‑bill and indirect reseller at the same time?

Yes — during the transition the profile shows both programs, the partner keeps billing existing direct customers and can simultaneously accept customers from the provider. Direct‑bill and indirect incentives can both be earned on the same tenant but are managed separately. Microsoft nonetheless recommends keeping the two businesses on two separate tenants long‑term, because enrolling direct CSP on an indirect reseller tenant overrides the indirect reseller features.

What exactly stops working in "Restricted" direct‑bill status?

Restricted means the partner is no longer eligible to transact as direct‑bill — growth motions stop (such as acquiring new customers and placing new direct orders) while existing subscriptions continue to run. The authoritative list is Microsoft's restricted direct‑bill capabilities page — review it before requesting an Active→Restricted change.

Is there a deadline to finish moving customers?

Microsoft's documentation notes that partners get a time frame for completing customer and subscription moves before losing the overlapping program access, and direct‑bill restriction itself follows Microsoft's compliance timelines. Treat the transition as a project with a target end date agreed with the provider — and confirm any applicable deadline for the specific account with Microsoft Partner Support.

Why does Partner‑of‑Record (POR) validation matter?

Since September 1, 2025, an indirect reseller must have an active CSP indirect reseller tenant and a verified PLA ID to be assigned as Partner of Record on transactions — orders are blocked at transaction time if validation fails (suspension, region mismatch, inactive PLA ID, or missing MPA acceptance). Completing Phase 2 properly is what keeps the partner transactable.

Glossary — every acronym in this guide

CSP — Cloud Solution Provider
Microsoft's program for partners that resell Microsoft cloud services.
Direct‑Bill Partner (Tier 1)
A CSP partner that buys directly from Microsoft and bills its customers itself.
Indirect Reseller (Tier 2)
A CSP partner that buys via an Indirect Provider; no direct billing relationship with Microsoft.
Indirect Provider
A distributor authorized by Microsoft to support indirect resellers — billing, ordering, support infrastructure.
Partner Center
Microsoft's portal (partner.microsoft.com) where all enrollment, customer and subscription actions happen.
PGA / PLA
Partner Global Account (the org‑level account) / Partner Location Account (a business location under it). Each location has its own PartnerID; CSP transactions are tied to a location's PLA ID.
PartnerID
The identifier generated for a partner location, used for CSP transactions and incentives mapping.
MAICPP — Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program
Microsoft's overall partner program; an active MAICPP enrollment is a prerequisite, and incentives invitations are tied to it.
MPA — Microsoft Partner Agreement
The binding agreement a partner accepts per CSP tenant. Accepting it is what enables indirect reseller capabilities; without it, the reseller can't transact with a distributor.
MCA — Microsoft Customer Agreement
The agreement every customer must accept; for this transition, acceptance must be recognized through the indirect provider.
GDAP — Granular Delegated Admin Permissions
Scoped, time‑bound admin access a customer grants the partner to manage their subscriptions, licenses and users.
NCE — New Commerce Experience
Microsoft's current commerce platform for CSP subscriptions. Only NCE license‑based and Azure‑plan subscriptions can be transferred between partners.
Azure plan
The NCE construct for Azure in CSP; classic ("legacy") Azure must migrate to Azure plan before transfer.
POR — Partner of Record
The reseller credited on a transaction; validated by Microsoft at transaction time since Sept 1, 2025.
Entra ID tenant
The Microsoft identity container (formerly Azure AD) a business runs in; a "new tenant" path means a fresh Entra directory for the reseller business.
Global admin / Admin agent
Partner Center roles: Global admin handles enrollment and agreements; Admin agent handles customer relationships and transfers.
PTC
Partner Technology/Transformation Consultant — the advisor guiding the partner through this SOP.
When you're stuck

Escalation paths
